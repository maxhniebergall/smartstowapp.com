How We Calculate Your Move

Unlike generic calculators that only count rooms, the SmartStow algorithm accounts for the density of your lifestyle. We use three specific data points to generate your estimate:

  • Volumetric Weight by Hobby: We assign specific volume metrics to complex hobbies. For example, a "Cyclist" profile adds significantly more volume (bikes, stands, parts) than a standard "Avg" profile.
  • Packing Velocity: We calculate labor hours based on a professional benchmark of 3-5 boxes packed per hour. If you are doing it yourself (DIY), expect to be on the lower end of this range.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many boxes do I need for a 3-bedroom house?

On average, a fully furnished 3-bedroom house requires between 70 and 100 medium-sized moving boxes. However, this varies wildly based on your lifestyle. A "Minimalist" household might only need 50, while a "Collector" could need 120+. Use the calculator above to adjust for your specific density.

What size moving truck should I rent?

For a standard 2-bedroom apartment, a 20' truck (approx. 1,000 cu ft) is the safest choice to avoid multiple trips. For 3+ bedrooms, move up to a 26' truck. Our calculator helps you decide by computing the total cubic footage of your furniture and boxes combined.

How long does it take to pack a house?

Packing is labor-intensive. Expect to spend roughly one hour for every 3-5 boxes. For a standard 3-bedroom home, this often totals 20-30 hours of pure packing labor. You can cut this time down by using voice-to-text inventory apps like SmartStow instead of writing manual lists.
