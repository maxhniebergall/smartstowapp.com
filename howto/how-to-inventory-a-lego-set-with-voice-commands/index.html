How to Inventory a LEGO Set with Voice Commands?

February 18, 2026 • 4 min read

To inventory a LEGO set, open SmartStow while online and speak the item details, including location and set number. This voice recording creates a digital log that you can search and view offline, providing a hands-free way to manage your hobby collection without manual typing.

Managing a growing LEGO collection requires precision and organization, especially when dealing with high-value sets like the Star Wars Ultimate Collector Series. Traditional methods often involve tedious manual entry that takes time away from the building process. SmartStow offers a modern solution by utilizing voice-to-text technology to catalog your hobby items efficiently. By speaking the details of your sets, you create a comprehensive digital library that is easy to navigate and maintain. This guide will walk you through the process of using voice commands to document your collection, ensuring every brick and minifigure is accounted for. Whether you are a casual builder or a serious investor, mastering voice-based inventory management will transform how you interact with your hobby. Using SmartStow allows you to focus on the joy of collecting while the app handles the logistical heavy lifting through simple vocal inputs.

The Quick Voice Command Method

  • Ensure your device has an active internet connection before beginning the recording process.
  • Open the SmartStow application on your mobile device.
  • Locate the voice recording trigger to initiate the logging sequence.
  • Speak clearly and at a natural pace to ensure the voice-to-text engine captures every detail.
  • Use a structured format for your command, such as: 'Top Shelf, LEGO Star Wars Millennium Falcon 75192, UCS.'
  • State the specific location first to help the system categorize the item immediately.
  • Include the brand name and the specific set name to differentiate between similar models.
  • Mention the unique set number, as this is the primary identifier for LEGO products.
  • Add any sub-series information, such as 'UCS' (Ultimate Collector Series), to provide context.
  • Review the transcribed text on your screen to confirm accuracy before saving the entry.
  • Once saved, the item is added to your digital inventory and becomes available for offline viewing.
