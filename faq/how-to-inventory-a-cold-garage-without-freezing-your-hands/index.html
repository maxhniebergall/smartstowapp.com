The Physical Friction of Spreadsheets in Cold Environments

The primary drawback of using spreadsheets in a garage is the requirement for fine motor skills. When your fingers are cold, your dexterity decreases, making typing on a keyboard or a small mobile screen frustrating and error-prone.

  • The Glove Problem: Most capacitive touchscreens require skin contact or specialized (and often unreliable) gloves. To type into a spreadsheet, you must remove your insulation, leading to rapid heat loss.
  • Device Temperature: Laptops and tablets are heat sinks. Holding a cold aluminum device while trying to navigate rows and columns is physically draining.
  • Hardware Vulnerability: Keyboards can become stiff in freezing temperatures, and the risk of dropping a device while fumbling with cold hands increases significantly.
  • Static Positioning: Spreadsheets often require you to sit or stand in one spot with a device, whereas voice recording allows you to keep moving, which helps maintain body heat.
